## Process Notes

To create this comic, I used an AI image generator to produce the visual panels and then organized them into a comic format. I started by thinking about the idea I wanted to explore: how AI imagines success and identity. Instead of uploading a real photo of myself, I asked the AI to generate an image of a “successful, wealthy, and friendly person.” My goal was to see what kinds of characteristics the AI would associate with those qualities.

After generating the first image, I reprompted the AI several times to adjust details and explore different versions. I then selected the images that best illustrated the idea for the comic. The final step was arranging the panels and writing the accompanying reflection to explain my interpretation of the images.

The main decisions I made were about the prompts, which images to keep, and how to interpret the results. Rather than accepting the AI’s output automatically, I focused on evaluating what the images suggested about how success is represented.

## Reflection
**What does “authenticity” mean when identity is co-constructed with AI?**

If identity is co-constructed with AI, authenticity becomes less about where something comes from and more about why and how it is used. Authenticity is not broken simply because AI is involved; it is broken when AI is allowed to speak instead of the user. For example, if a student submits an AI-generated reflection without reading, revising, or questioning it, the work no longer represents their thinking. In that case, the ideas, values, and judgments belong to the AI, not the student. The user becomes a passive transmitter rather than an author.

In contrast, authenticity is preserved when AI functions as a tool rather than a replacement for thought. For example, a student might ask AI to generate multiple ways to explain a complex idea, then select one version, revise the language, remove parts that feel inaccurate, and add personal insight. As long as we control what to discard, what to reshape, and what to keep, authorship remains ours.
